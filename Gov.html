State-wise Subsidies in India

About Government Subsidies

The Government of India under the leadership of Prime Minister Narendra Modi has launched several subsidy schemes to support farmers, poor households, and small industries. These subsidies aim to reduce the financial burden, promote modern farming, ensure food security, and encourage sustainable development.

  • PM-KISAN Samman Nidhi: Direct income support of ₹6,000 per year to small and marginal farmers.
  • Fertilizer Subsidy: Financial support to farmers for affordable fertilizers to boost crop productivity.
  • Ujjwala Yojana: Subsidy on LPG connections to poor households for clean cooking fuel.
  • PM Kusum Yojana: Subsidy for solar-powered irrigation pumps and renewable energy in agriculture.
  • Food Subsidy (NFSA): Affordable food grains to over 80 crore people through ration shops.
  • Ayushman Bharat - PMJAY: Subsidized health insurance for economically weaker families.
  • Housing Subsidy (PMAY): Subsidy on home loans for affordable housing under Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana.

These initiatives reflect the government’s focus on empowering farmers, improving rural livelihoods, and ensuring inclusive growth across India.
